## Question 6
**Question:** Walk me through what happens when a box arrives at the fill station.
**Model:** anthropic:claude-sonnet-4-6
**Time:** 27.08s

**Answer:**
Here's the sequence of events when a box arrives at the fill station in Auto mode:

1. **PhotoEye_Fill goes TRUE** — the photoeye detects the box at the fill station.
2. **Box_At_Fill latches TRUE** — but only if a fill isn't already in progress and the box wasn't already registered. This prevents double-triggering.
3. **Conveyor stops** — because Box_At_Fill is now TRUE, the conveyor logic at the bottom of AutoMode sees that a fill is in progress and sets Conveyor_Run to FALSE.
4. **Fill_In_Progress goes TRUE and Fill_Timer starts** — the routine kicks off the fill cycle and starts the 5-second timeout clock.
5. **Fill_Valve opens (TRUE)** — product starts dispensing into the box.
6. **Two things are now being watched simultaneously:**
   - **Level_Sensor goes TRUE (normal completion)** — the box is full. Fill_Valve closes, Fill_In_Progress and Box_At_Fill both clear, Fill_Timer resets, and the Conveyor_Delay_Timer starts its 500ms countdown before the conveyor restarts.
   - **Fill_Timer times out at 5000ms (fault path)** — if the level sensor never triggered, Fill_Valve closes, the fill flags clear, Fault_Active goes TRUE with Fault_Code 3, and Rejected_Box_Count increments by 1. The line faults out at that point.
7. **Conveyor restarts (normal path only)** — after the 500ms Conveyor_Delay_Timer completes, Conveyor_Run goes back TRUE and the box moves on down the line.

The key safety here is the fill timeout — if a box never fills properly within 5 seconds, the line won't just keep running; it faults and counts the rejection so you know something went wrong.

## Question 10
**Question:** What happens if a box is at the fill station but never gets full?
**Model:** anthropic:claude-sonnet-4-6
**Time:** 24.78s

**Answer:**
If the fill timer runs for 5 seconds (its preset) without the Level_Sensor ever going TRUE, the Fill_Timer fires its done bit and AutoMode treats that as a timeout.

Here's what happens in sequence:

1. Fill_Valve is turned off — product stops dispensing.
2. Fill_In_Progress and Box_At_Fill are both cleared to FALSE — the fill station resets as if the cycle is over.
3. Fault_Active is set TRUE and Fault_Code is set to 3 — the line goes into fault.
4. Rejected_Box_Count increments by 1 — the box is counted as a reject.
5. Because Fault_Active is now TRUE, MainRoutine jumps straight to FaultHandler and StackLightControl, skipping normal operation.
6. FaultHandler kills the conveyor and fill valve (already off), and retracts the seal cylinder if it's not already home.
7. StackLightControl turns the red stack light on and kills green and yellow.

The line will stay faulted until an operator presses the HMI Reset Button **and** the E-stop is cleared and the safety gate is confirmed closed. Note that the under-filled box is not automatically removed — it just gets counted as rejected, so someone will need to deal with it physically.
